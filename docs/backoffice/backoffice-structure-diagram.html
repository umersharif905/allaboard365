Back Office Structure — Share Requests, Cases & Encounters

The vendor portal tracks three separate objects. This map shows what each one is, how it is categorized, the statuses it moves through, and — most importantly — how they do and don't connect.

Share Request
Case
Encounter
Vendor — the organization › Member — the patient

Every object below is scoped to a Vendor and rooted to a Member. One member can hold many Share Requests, many Cases, and many Encounters at the same time.

01

Share Request

A member's medical event moving through the sharing / reimbursement workflow.
Starts from a member's request form
Type — per-vendor configurable · clinical medical-event categories
Surgery — Inpatient Surgery — Outpatient Procedure Treatment Maternity
Workflow Status
New→ Acknowledged→ In Review→ Awaiting Member Info/ Awaiting Authorization→ Processing→ Completed
Closed without completion: DeniedWithdrawn
Determination — a separate outcome axis
Pending Eligible Not Eligible Undetermined
Carries financial & clinical data — bills, providers, diagnoses, procedures, payments and documents.
Not Linked

Separate & independent. A Share Request is never linked to a Case — no connection, no conversion. They are two parallel tracks.

02

Case

A back-office support / work item — escalations, billing problems, complaints, appeals. No financial determination workflow.
Created by vendor staff — never from a member form
Type & Subcategory — per-vendor configurable
Reimbursement OON CopayPreventative Other
Billing Provider InvoiceNegotiation RecoveryClaims / COB
Encounter Escalation Needs Follow UpIssue Raised Routed to Team
Complaint Service QualityProcess / Outcome Privacy
Appeals Denied ShareDenied Reimbursement Amount Dispute2nd Level
Status
Open→ In Progress→ Pending→ Closed
Carries operational records — notes, providers and documents. No bills, amounts or determination.
Optional pin → Share Request Optional pin → Case
03

Encounter

A logged record of a conversation with a member — a call, email, or visit. The first-class place to capture what was said.
Logged by the care team · the connective thread
An Encounter can be optionally pinned to a Share Request, to a Case, to both, or to neither. It is the only object that touches both tracks.
↳ Share Request only ↳ Case only ↳ Both ↳ Neither = Triage

An Encounter can be escalated → converted into a Case.
The takeaway: Share Requests and Cases never connect to each other. Encounters are the only thread that can touch both — or sit on their own in Triage.
